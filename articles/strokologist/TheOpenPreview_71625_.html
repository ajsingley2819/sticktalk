Dr. Strokologist’s Preview: Rain, Rough, and Repeats at Royal Portrush

Welcome to Northern Ireland — where the wind bites, the Guinness flows, and the golf gods laugh at your hopes and dreams.

The Open Championship returns to Royal Portrush, a linksland masterpiece tucked into the cliffs of County Antrim.

Last time we were here in 2019, Shane Lowry went full hometown hero and won by six strokes, finishing at -15… even with a Sunday 73. Legend.

Tommy Fleetwood? He finished second. Not close, but second. We remember, Tommy. We remember.

And while I know I always say I love The Masters… this is my favorite tournament of the year.
Something about the early morning tee times, the moody Irish skies, and a strong cup of coffee while watching the world’s best get humbled by the wind. IT FIRES ME UP!

***Maybe even add a splash of whiskey to the coffee on the weekend. It’s The Open. It’s tradition.

Course Breakdown:

Royal Portrush Golf Club

  • Par: 71
  • Yardage: 7,381 yards
  • Purse: $17,000,000
  • Terrain: Rolling dunes, unpredictable weather, and greens that’ll have you questioning your life choices.

Iconic Holes at Royal Portrush

Hole 5 – White Rocks (Par 4, 374 yards)
A stunner. Coastal views so dramatic they could win a BAFTA. But don’t let the scenery fool you, one bad swing and your ball’s cliff-diving into the Atlantic like it’s in an episode of Love Island: Belfast.

Hole 16 – Calamity Corner (Par 3, 236 yards)
Sounds like a Marvel villain. Plays like one too. No bailout left, 236 yards of sheer terror, and wind that’ll make your Pro V1 question its career. If your ball lands on the green, consider buying a lottery ticket… you're hot.

Hole 17 – Purgatory (Par 4, 408 yards)
You just survived Calamity… and now this? A tight fairway, blind shots, and a green that rolls like it was built by spiteful leprechauns. They don’t call it Purgatory for nothing. You’re stuck between golf heaven and hell.

Hole 18 – Babington’s (Par 4, 474 yards)
A worthy closer with OB left, rough right, and a crowd waiting to judge you in eerie Northern Irish silence. Want to win The Open? Better bring your A-game and your therapist.

Fun Facts:

  • Founded in 1888, Royal Portrush is the only course in Northern Ireland to host The Open.
  • Hosted twice: 1951 (won by Max Faulkner, who rocked a tie while playing. We should bring this back) and 2019 (Lowry-palooza).

Final Word:

There’s a video of Scottie Scheffler going around that hit golf Twitter like a Bryson drive on a hole going downhill with tailwind. In it, he says:

“I love putting in the work. I love getting to practice. I love getting to live out my dreams. But at the end of the day, sometimes I just don’t understand the point.”

If that doesn’t rattle your Sunday scaries, I don’t know what will. The best player in the world just hit us with a spiritual fade to the heart.

No temper tantrum, no club toss. Just a man who’s got all the trophies, a supporting wife and young child, and a bank account that makes LIV guys jealous… asking “what’s even the point?”

And honestly? Same, Scottie. I write this column every major, drink three cold brews before noon, and have a futures ticket on guys +7000 or higher every other week… what is the point?

Hey Rory, take notes. This is how you do introspection without needing a press embargo or a PR firm on speed dial (sorry for the dig Bryson).

Scottie didn’t say it outright, but the takeaway is clear: trophies don’t fill the tank. The man’s playing for something bigger than purses and podcasts.

And yes, you still should never bet against him. Even when he’s asking existential questions mid-dominance.
